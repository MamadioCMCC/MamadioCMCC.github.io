Mattia Amadio, PhD

Disastrologist and map ennthusiast


Since my master thesis, my research has focused on estimating the economic risk triggered by natural hazards and, consequently, understanding the relationships between climate change and the frequency of extreme events. a flood risk modelling framework which covers the spatial representation of economic value, the characterisation of hazard magnitude in relation to climate variables, and the elaboration of updated, reliable damage models. He runs the climate service FLOOD-MAGE, which is a tool for the estimation of economic costs triggered by flood events.

Bachelor and Master Degree

My bachelor is in "Environmental Sciences", a multi-disciplinary three-years degree held at Ca'Foscari University of Venice.

After that, I got a joint-degree master in "Sustainable Development" with a six month period spent at the Utrecht Universiteit, in the Department of Geography (EGEA).

My master thesis "Looking forward from the past" is related to flood damage modelling in reference to an extreme event of the past, and it was developed during an internship at Fondazione Eni Enrico Mattei.
