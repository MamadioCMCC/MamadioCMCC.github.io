Mattia Amadio, PhD

Disastrologist and map ennthusiast


Post-doc researcher at CMCC foundation

Since my master thesis, my research has focused on estimating the economic risk triggered by natural hazards and, consequently, understanding the relationships between climate change and the frequency of extreme events.

My research activity have been dedicated in large part to flood risk in different place of the Earth.
My objective have been do develop a spatial-explicit framework for risk assessment which could be practically employed for cost estimation of flood risk. This research culminated in the release of the climate service FLOOD-MAGE.

Bachelor and Master Degree

My bachelor is in Environmental Sciences, a multi-disciplinary three-years degree held at Ca'Foscari University of Venice.

After that, I got a joint-degree master in Sustainable Development with a six month period spent at the Utrecht Universiteit, in the Department of Geography.

My master thesis Looking forward from the past is related to flood damage modelling in reference to an extreme event of the past, and it was developed during an internship at research center Fondazione Eni Enrico Mattei.






Doctorate Degree

From 2015 to 2018 I carried out my PhD in Science and management of Climate Change at the Ca' Foscari University defending a thesis titled Integrated framework for flood risk assessment in Italy. The thesis (open access) consists of four papers published on peer-reviewed, non-predatory online journals.

I spent two semester abroad as a visiting PhD student:

  • one semester at the Integrated Catchment Assessment and Management center (iCAM), Australian National University, under the supervision of Prof. Tony Jakeman;
  • one semester at the Institute for Environmental Studies (IVM), Amsterdam Vrije Univeriteit, under the supervision of Prof. Philip Ward.
